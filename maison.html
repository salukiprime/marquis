La Maison

A legacy of uncompromising excellence and avant-garde design since 1928.

[ 1928: The Genesis ]

The Genesis

Founded by visionary engineer Alexandre Marquis in the heart of Europe, the brand began with a simple yet ambitious goal: to create the most dynamically capable and luxurious vehicles the world had ever seen.

The early models defied traditional constraints, setting sweeping records on the track while redefining sheer elegance for grand touring on the open road.

[ 1968: The Golden Era ]

The Golden Era

Throughout the mid-century, Marquis became unmistakably synonymous with cinematic glamour and high society. The introduction of the iconic Scarlet emblem marked a shift towards bold, aggressive aesthetics.

Every vehicle rolling out of La Maison was recognized not just as an assembly of mechanical parts, but as a kinetic sculpture built purely to evoke emotion.

[ Today: Electric Excellence ]

Electric Excellence

Today, Marquis leads the charge into the sustainable future of absolute luxury. By integrating state-of-the-art electric powertrains without sacrificing an ounce of its legendary comfort or raw performance, the brand remains at the pinnacle of the automotive industry.

Models like the Belvédère and Mistral prove that silent running and zero emissions are the ultimate forms of modern indulgence.
